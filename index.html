MAP: 20$ dinners in New York City

Because fine dining is overrated—and your wallet agrees.

By Rambo Talabong

In a city where the cost of a meal can leave your wallet crying for mercy, Hell Gate's "$20 Dinner" series is here to save you.

This map shows all the places Hell Gate's Scott Lynch has hit up for a decent dinner under $20.

From hole-in-the-wall joints to neighborhood gems, the series is about finding the soul of New York food without emptying your pockets—because let’s be real, rent’s already doing that.

The map not only gives you a handy way to find these spots but also lets you scope out which neighborhoods are down with affordable eats. Whether it's a greasy slice in Brooklyn, a loaded taco in Queens, or a bowl of something hot in Manhattan, this list proves that you don’t need a trust fund to eat well in New York City.

Hell Gate's not just about cheap eats—it’s about celebrating the scrappy, no-frills food culture that makes New York, well, New York. In a city where Michelin stars and overpriced small plates reign supreme, sometimes the best food is in the places where they just give a damn about flavor.

This map is your ticket to Hell Gate's journey through affordable dinners in NYC.

Whether you’re a broke student, a budget-conscious family, or just someone who’s tired of $40 entrees, Hell Gate's "$20 Dinner" series is for you. Let the map lead you to your next favorite spot—proof that, yes, you can still eat well in this city without blowing a paycheck.
